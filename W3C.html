About W3C

The World Wide Web Consortium (W3C) is an international community where Member organizations, a full-time staff, and the public work together to develop Web standards. Led by Web inventor Sir Tim Berners-Lee and CEO Jeffrey Jaffe, W3C's mission is to lead the Web to its full potential.

Mission and Goals

W3C's mission is to develop open standards that ensure the long-term growth of the Web. W3C strives to make the Web accessible, interoperable, and useful for everyone.

Web Standards

W3C develops and maintains a wide range of Web standards, including HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and more. These standards ensure consistency and compatibility across different browsers and devices.

History

W3C was founded in 1994 by Sir Tim Berners-Lee and others, with the goal of creating a common platform for innovation on the Web. Since its inception, W3C has played a vital role in shaping the evolution of the Web.

Achievements

Over the years, W3C has achieved significant milestones, including the development of HTML5, CSS3, and various accessibility guidelines. These achievements have contributed to a more dynamic and inclusive Web experience.

Ongoing Initiatives

W3C continues to lead and collaborate on various initiatives, such as Web Accessibility, Semantic Web, and Web of Things. These initiatives address emerging challenges and opportunities on the Web.

Members

W3C's members include a diverse group of organizations from around the world, collaborating to shape the future of the Web. These members contribute to the development and adoption of Web standards.
